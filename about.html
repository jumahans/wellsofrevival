Our Story

Transforming Lives Through Christ's Love

Our History & Mission

The wells of revival Ministry came into being as as result of Gode speaking to Rev. John Ayisi and his wife Pastor Joyce Ayisi. The first sunday service was held on 24th Nov 2001.

The Ministry was born out of a call of god uponHis servant to raise upmen and women of integrity & character who will be a beginning of a new generation wherever they are.

Overturning the purpose of the enemy through spiritual spiritual warfare, changing the history and destiny of their people and becoming an instrument of strength and encouragementto God's people throught strategic praying and declaration of the word in the name of jesusChrist of Nazareth.

This becomes the hub of God's will and purpose for the ministry. A clear vision was realized, recognizing that the primary mandate for the mission of the church was to evangelize, teach and train God called workers.

the driving force behind the siccessof the wells of revival ministry has been prayer and the preaching of the solid word of God and the naked cross of Jesus Christ.

Over the years, we have witnessed countless lives transformed through the power of God's Word and the fellowship of believers. Our congregation has grown not just in numbers but in spiritual depth and community impact.

What We Believe

Our Foundation of Faith

Tenets of Faith

God the Creator

We believe in God the creator of heaven and earth and all therein. (Gen 1:1-2, Ex 20:11, 1 Sam 2:8-2, 2 Kings 19:6, 1 Chron 16:26)

The Trinity

We believe in God consisting of the Father, Son and Holy Spirit (Trinity). (2 Cor 13:14)

Jesus Christ

We believe in the deity of Jesus Christ, in His virgin birth, in His atoning death, in His bodily resurrection, in His ascension to the right hand of the Father. (1 Tim 3:16)

The Bible

We believe in the Bible as the inspired, infallible and authoritative word of God. (2 Peter 1:21)

Salvation

We believe that man was created in the image of God, fell from the glory of God through sin and therefore needs salvation. (Gen 1:2, Rom 3:26, Rom 5)

Holy Spirit Baptism

We believe in the baptism of the Holy Spirit and the manifestation, gifts, ministries and fruit of the Spirit in life. (Acts 2:4, Acts 10:46, Acts 19:16, 1 Cor 12:8-11, Gal 5:22-23)

Divine Protection

We believe in the protection and security of God for every believer who shall maintain a holy walk in the Spirit. (Jude 2, Rom 8:4)

Divine Healing

We believe in divine healing which is obtained on the basis of the atonement. (Ps 103:3, James 5:14-16, 1 Peter 2:24)

Second Coming

We believe in the bodily return of our Saviour and Lord Jesus Christ in great power and glory at the close of the present age. (Dan 7:14, Acts 1:11, 1 Thess 4:13-18)

Resurrection

We believe in the resurrection of both the saved and the lost. The saved unto eternal life and the lost unto eternal punishment. (John 5:28-29, 1 Cor 15:51-55, 1 Thess 4:13-18)

Marriage

We believe in the sanctity of marriage and the biblical view on monogamy (one man, one wife) as the ideal form of marriage. (Gen 2:24, Matt 5:27, Mark 10:7-9, Rom 7:2, 1 Cor 7:10-11, Heb 13:4)

Child Dedication

We believe in the dedication of infants by the laying of hands. (1 Sam 1:24-28)

Lord's Supper

We believe in the fellowship of the Lord's Supper. (Matt 26:26, Mark 14:22, Luke 22:19-20, 1 Cor 10:16, 1 Cor 11:23)

Spiritual Authority

We believe in God as the supreme power and authority over the heavens and the earth and that He has delegated power and authority through our Lord Jesus Christ to believers who can now claim victory over Satan and demonic forces of darkness. (Luke 10:19, Mark 6:7, Mark 16:17)

Scope of Authority

1

We believe that the kingdom of God's government is theocratic, Christ being sovereign and reigning head who builds, governs and sets her in order through leadership ministry of apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors and teachers.

2

We believe the church must have a person of Christ's appointing as the spiritual leader as discerned by the church leadership.

3

We know that democracy is not an appropriate form of government for the New Testament church as it is not supported by God's pattern shown in the Holy Scriptures.

4

Leaders shall be appointed according to gifting, grace and calling. This shall be done by the relevant authorities as stipulated in this document.

5

We pursue family covenant relationships which we believe to be the true pattern for New Testament church. There are three (3) ministry structures in the Wells of Revival Ministry:

  • Local church structure
  • Regional council
  • National council

Principle of Autonomy

We hold as Scripture and fundamental the principle of local church government by men and women of character and integrity according to (1 Tim 3, Titus 1:5-9) that is, the local church is sovereign under Christ's headship and all ecclesiastical power is exercised by each one of these churches assembled as a congregation.

Decisions made must be according to the biblical principles, this ministers manual and the constitution.
